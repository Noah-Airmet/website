About

I'm a cybersecurity student at BYU and a junior developer at Simplicity Group, where I get to build web applications with AI-assisted tools like Cursor and Claude every day with a fantastic, forward-thinking team. I'm super excited about what these tools make possible and that's exactly why I think governance matters. The organizations that figure out how to use AI confidently and responsibly are the ones that will actually capture its full value.

But right now, most haven't answered the hard questions: whether AI-generated code is introducing security vulnerabilities, what proprietary data is being exposed to third-party models, and who's accountable when something goes wrong.

My experience is shaping the career I'm building. I'm focused on the intersection of cybersecurity GRC and AI governance, grounded in hands-on technical work and a serious understanding of the frameworks that matter — NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, the EU AI Act, and the growing patchwork of U.S. state AI legislation.

I believe the strongest AI governance professionals will be the ones who understand the technology from the inside. That's what I'm building toward.
